How each column is computed
Gross — $1 − ask(YES on venue A) − ask(NO on venue B), in cents per $1 contract pair. Positive = you can buy both sides for less than the guaranteed $1 payout. Prices are depth-adjusted VWAPs where order books are available.
Net (retail) — Gross minus: venue fees (Kalshi 0.07·P·(1−P); Polymarket taker fee where enabled) and Blinq retail costs on the levered leg: 10bps open + 10bps close + 50bps vig per side + 43.8% APR borrow on the borrowed ⅔ of notional for the expected hold.
Net (MM) — Same, but Blinq MM/arb tier: 5bps per side, no vig, 12% APR borrow. The gap between the two columns is the fee-tier argument.
Ann. ROC (MM) — Net (MM) ÷ capital deployed (spot-leg premium + levered-leg margin = notional/leverage), × 365 ÷ expected hold. Annualization amplifies small numbers in both directions — read it with the hold length.
Hold — min(your input, days to each leg's resolution). Borrow cost scales with this; closing early on convergence improves the levered leg.
Depth — DEPTH OK: spread survives the selected fill size on both books · TOB ONLY: top-of-book quotes, depth unverified · ARTIFACT: one leg's bid-ask wider than 5¢, so the "spread" is book width, not price disagreement (excluded from ranking).

Method

Gross spread = $1 − ask(YES, venue A) − ask(NO, venue B), tested both directions with the Blinq-levered leg on Polymarket. Depth-adjusted VWAPs from live books (PM CLOB, Kalshi orderbook); otherwise top-of-book, flagged TOB.

Blinq Composite = Σ wᵢ·midᵢ, PM anchored 60%, remainder split by visible depth; stale/one-sided venues dropped. Venue deviation from composite is the rich/cheap signal — the same imbalance that spikes Blinq skew funding on the minority side (funding receipts, not modeled, further offset borrow).

History methodology: Kalshi hourly candlesticks (yes bid/ask closes) joined to PM hourly price points within ±90 min. PM executable prices approximated as price ± a configurable half-spread (PM history exposes trades/mids, not books). Episodes = consecutive arb hours. Daily-granularity caution: this measures standing divergence; sub-hour flickers are invisible, so counts are conservative.

Not modeled: skew-funding receipts (kicker in the arb's favor), gas, depth beyond the walk, margin-path liquidation risk on the levered leg, Limitless taker fees, and contract fine-print differences — see equivalence notes. A spread is sometimes the fine print, not free money.
