Market detail · Blinq Composite

Per-venue executable prices, deviation from the composite, and depth. Composite = candidate Blinq mark price: depth-weighted mid, PM-anchored, stale venues dropped.

Method

Gross spread = $1 − ask(YES, venue A) − ask(NO, venue B), tested in both directions across every venue pair with the Blinq-levered leg on Polymarket. Prices are depth-adjusted: the VWAP to fill the selected size on each leg, walked from live order books where the venue exposes one (Polymarket CLOB, Kalshi orderbook); otherwise top-of-book, flagged TOB.

Net edge subtracts: Kalshi taker fee (0.07·P·(1−P)), Polymarket taker fee where enabled (rate·P·(1−P) from the market's fee schedule), and Blinq costs on the levered leg — retail tier: 10bps open + 10bps close + 50bps vig per side + 43.8% APR borrow on the borrowed portion for the expected hold; MM tier: 5bps per side, no vig, 12% APR borrow. Capital = spot leg premium + levered leg margin (notional/leverage). Annualized = ROC × 365 / hold days.

Blinq Composite = Σ wᵢ·midᵢ with Polymarket anchored at 60% and the remainder split by visible depth; venues with quotes older than the scan or with no two-sided book are dropped. Deviation of a venue leg from the composite is the rich/cheap signal — the same imbalance that spikes Blinq skew funding on the minority side (funding receipts, not modeled here, further offset borrow cost).

Not modeled: skew-funding receipts (kicker in the arb's favor), gas, slippage beyond the depth walk, margin-path liquidation risk on the levered leg (run below max leverage), and contract fine-print differences — see per-market equivalence notes. Spreads across venues are sometimes the fine print, not free money. Verify rules before trading.
